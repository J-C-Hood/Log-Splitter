A TWO-STAGE PUMP

 

  •  Is an ingenious time-saver
  •  Contains two pumping sections
  •  Internal pressure-sensing valve that switches over between the two sections
  • One section of the pump generates the maximum gpm flow rate at a lower pressure. It is used, for example, to draw the piston back out of a log after the log has been split.
  •  Drawing the piston back into the cylinder takes very little force and you want it to happen quickly, so you want the highest possible flow rate at low pressure.
  • When pushing the piston into a log, however, you want the highest possible pressure in order to generate the maximum splitting force.
  •  The flow rate isn't a big concern, so the pump switches to its "high pressure, lower volume" stage to split the log.
